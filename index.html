PCI DSS · PhorestPay

Scoping the CDE

Constraining PCI scope to the payment page environment

Discussion document · April 2026

Level set

Where We're Aligned

We're aligned that PhorestPay is being treated as a service provider and that we'll need to go through a RoC. We're not trying to challenge that.

The key thing we want to get right is the definition of the Cardholder Data Environment — because that's what will determine the scale of the work across engineering.

There is a very big difference between a tightly scoped payment surface and pulling large parts of our application and infrastructure into scope.

02 · architecture
Our position · the anchor

Constrain Scope to the Payment Page

We want to constrain scope to the payment page environment — rather than extending it across the broader application and backend systems.

✗ Broad scope

CDE expanded across the stack:

  • Entire Next.js application
  • Backend services & APIs
  • Supporting infrastructure
  • Large parts of engineering pulled in

✓ Constrained scope

CDE bounded to what serves the page:

  • The payment page surface
  • Script delivery & integrity
  • Strong controls around that surface
  • Backend remains out of scope
03 · position
Industry alignment

Consistent With What We're Seeing

This is consistent with what we're seeing from other SaaS companies with similar architectures and their QSAs, where scope is constrained to the payment page rather than the full application.

Pattern

Hosted iframes

Card capture outsourced to a PCI-validated provider via iframes or SDK.

Pattern

Page-level CDE

CDE defined at the page that loads the iframe — not the whole app.

Pattern

Backend out

Orchestration services (e.g. PaymentIntent creation) remain out of scope.

04 · industry
PCI DSS 4.x

Where the Standard Is Moving

The focus has shifted more towards securing the payment page environment — script integrity, tamper prevention, monitoring — rather than broadly expanding scope where card data is fully outsourced.

6.4.3

Script control

Scripts on the payment page are inventoried, authorised, and integrity-assured.

11.6.1

Tamper detection

Change and tamper detection on the page — headers and script contents.

Direction

Targeted, not broad

Controls focus on the consumer-facing page, not the full backend.

This is the same direction we want our scope to take — strong controls where the page lives, not scope sprawl across the stack.

05 · pci 4.x
Our commitment

Strong Controls Around the Page

We're not trying to avoid responsibility. We're proposing to put strong controls around the payment page.

1

Strict script control

Allowlisted scripts, inventoried, version-pinned, integrity-verified.

2

Restricted access & deployment

Tight change control on anything that touches the payment page.

3

Monitoring & tamper detection

Runtime integrity checks, header monitoring, alerting on drift.

4

Regular testing & validation

Penetration testing, script review, continuous validation of the surface.

06 · controls
Option we're evaluating

Stripe-Hosted Checkout

Option A · fallback

Moving to Stripe-hosted Checkout would remove our application from hosting the payment page entirely and materially reduce scope.

Option B · preferred — confirm first

Before going down that route, we'd like to understand whether we can achieve a similarly constrained scope with our current Elements-based approach.

Both paths lead to a tightly scoped CDE. The question we want to answer together is which path the QSA is comfortable with.

07 · options
Technical proposal

What We're Proposing

Frontend · in scope

Isolated payment page

Isolate the payment page within the Next.js frontend as a controlled environment, with Stripe Elements handling all cardholder data capture.

Backend · out of scope

Orchestration only

Backend services act only as orchestration (e.g. PaymentIntent creation) and do not influence how cardholder data is captured or transmitted.

CDE = the isolated payment page + its controls. Everything else stays out of scope by design.
